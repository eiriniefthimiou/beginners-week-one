the founders:

  • Steve Jobs

Jobs went to Reed College for a semester and then dropped out. He spent 18 more months dropping in on more creative classes. Steve was a businessman most famous for his work with the company Apple Computer Inc, starting with the release of the Apple I in 1976.Together with Apple co-founder Steve Wozniak, Jobs helped make the idea of the personal computer popular in the late 1970s. In the early 1980s, still at Apple, Jobs was one of the first to see the potential of using a mouse to control things on a computer screen. In August 2011, Jobs resigned, and was appointed Chairman of Apple. He served in that position until he died, at the age of 56, on October 5, 2011 from complications of pancreatic cancer.

  • Steve Wozniak

Steven Gary Wozniak is an American computer engineer from San Jose, California. In the 1970s, he and Steve Jobs founded Apple. Wozniak designed the Apple I and Apple II computers and left Apple in the 1980s, but still owns Apple stock. He has founded several other Silicon Valley companies and now holds the patent for the microcomputer.

  • Ronald Gerald Wayne

Ronald Gerald Wayne is a retired American electronics industry worker. He co-founded Apple Computer with Steve Wozniak and Steve Jobs. Wayne drew the first Apple logo. He created the partnership agreement. He also wrote the Apple I manual. After two weeks he gave up his share of the new company for a total of $800 USD. His 10% share would be worth 60 billion today.
